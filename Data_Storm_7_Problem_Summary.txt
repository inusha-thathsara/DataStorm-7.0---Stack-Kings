# Data Storm 7.0 - Storming Round Problem Summary

## 1. Background & Business Context
* **Organization:** Organized by the Rotaract Club of University of Moratuwa and powered by OCTAVE (John Keells Group).
* **Business Problem:** A leading beverage manufacturer in Sri Lanka with over 80,000 traditional retail outlets (urban grocery stores, rural "kades"/corner shops, eateries, pharmacies) wants to shift its resource allocation (trade marketing budgets, coolers, promotional discounts) from a historical sales-average model to a **Potential-Based Allocation** model.
* **The Flaw in History:** Historical sales only reflect what an outlet *did* sell, not what it *could* sell, due to underperformance driven by stock management issues, credit constraints, or supply caps.
* **Objective:** Predict the **Maximum Monthly Purchase Potential** (latent maximum monthly volume potential in liters) for each traditional trade outlet for the month of **January 2026**.
* **Current Scope:** Focuses on **20,000 traditional trade outlets** serviced by **10 key distributors** across **4 key provinces** in Sri Lanka:
    * *Western Province:* DIST_W_01, DIST_W_02, DIST_W_03 (3 distributors)
    * *Central Province:* DIST_C_01, DIST_C_02, DIST_C_03 (3 distributors)
    * *North-Western Province:* DIST_NW_01, DIST_NW_02 (2 distributors)
    * *Southern Province:* DIST_S_01, DIST_S_02 (2 distributors)

## 2. Preliminary Problem Statement & Data Challenges
* **The Latent Demand:** "Potential" is a theoretical ceiling and a hidden variable. There is no perfect ground truth or target variable ($y$). 
* **Censored Data:** Historical volume is right-censored, representing the minimum of:
    * (A) True consumer demand.
    * (B) Systemic Constraints (credit limits, stockouts, delivery caps).
* **Data Quality Reality:** Datasets are raw exports from legacy Sales Force Automation (SFA) and distributor ERP systems. They contain legacy system artifacts, connectivity blackouts, human data-entry shortcuts, automated ghost entries, and master-data decay. Data forensics is required to isolate true market signals.

## 3. Provided & External Datasets
### Provided Datasets:
1.  `transactions_history_final.csv`: Granular outlet-level data.
2.  `outlet_master.csv`: Outlet-related data including Longitude and Latitude.
3.  `distributor_seasonality_details.csv`: Month-specific seasonality details for distributors.
4.  `holiday_list.csv`: List of holidays.

### External Data Challenge (POI Scraping):
* No Point of Interest (POI) data is provided. Teams must use public APIs (e.g., OpenStreetMap, Overpass) or web scraping to acquire external geospatial signals (schools, bus stands, hospitals, tourist attractions) to establish catchment area potential.

## 4. Technical & Architectural Requirements
### 4.1 Lakehouse Pipeline Architecture
Teams must structure code into three distinct layers (local directory folders/files are acceptable):
* **Bronze (Raw Ingestion):** Flat files ingested as-is with zero transformations to preserve original data state.
* **Silver (Cleaned):** Apply Data Engineering (DE) checks and cleaning logic. Failing records must be sent to a separate **quarantined/rejected records store** with documented failure reasons—never silently dropped.
* **Gold (Enriched):** Feature engineering and model-ready outputs combining cleaned internal data and externally scraped POI data.

### 4.2 Reusable Data Quality Checks
DE checks must be implemented as reusable, parameterizable functions applied consistently across all datasets:
* **Duplicate Check:** Detect duplicate records based on configurable primary keys (single/composite).
* **Null Check:** Flag records where mandatory fields contain null or empty values.
* **Referential Integrity Check:** Validate that foreign key values exist in reference datasets.
* **Value Range Check:** Assert numeric fields fall within expected min/max boundaries.
* **Format/Type Check:** Validate fields conform to expected data types/formats (e.g., dates, IDs).

## 5. Deliverables
1.  **Latent Potential Output (CSV):** Named `teamname_predictions.csv` containing columns `Outlet_ID` and predicted `Maximum_Monthly_Liters` for January 2026.
2.  **Reproducible Codebase (GitHub/Zipped Repo):** Must include a `README.md` with pipeline instructions, a codebase structured into Bronze/Silver/Gold layers, and analytical/modeling pipelines.
3.  **PDF Summary Report (Max 5 pages):** Technical document addressing:
    * *Data Forensics and Hygiene:* System anomalies trapped, quarantine setup, and DE check architecture.
    * *POI Data Acquisition:* API/scraping approach, targeted catchment drivers, and mapping methodology.
    * *Causal Base Logic:* Statistical/probabilistic framework for solving the left-censored demand curve and calculation of the uncapped ceiling.
    * *GenAI Transparency Log:* Log of how, where, and why LLMs (Gemini, ChatGPT, Copilot) were utilized as engineering accelerators, including iterative prompting and validation steps.

## 6. Evaluation Metrics (Qualitative - 100%)
* **Data Engineering and Forensics (40%):** Evaluation of the Bronze-Silver-Gold pipeline, reusable checks, quarantine implementation, legacy artifact neutralization, web-scraping pipelines, and feature engineering.
* **Methodology and Base Math (40%):** Conceptualization of "Latent Potential" and the statistical/mathematical robustness in handling missing target variables and right-censored data.
* **Generative AI Utilization and Workflow (20%):** Assessment of the GenAI transparency log, verifying intelligent acceleration (brainstorming, writing boilerplate code, debugging) versus blind utilization.
